## 1.1	Background of the Study 

Disability is part of being human and is integral to the human experience. Globally, persons with disabilities and limited mobility conditions like stroke, spinal cord injuries, and cerebral palsy encounter diverse physical environmental accessibility challenges in their daily lives. Several forms of physical barriers exist in built environment such as homes, schools, hospitals, workplaces, shopping malls, restaurants, and other facilities. About 16% of the global population live with significant disabilities and limited mobility. In Ghana, the rate of physical disability is estimated to be between 3% and 4%. The landscape of Assistive Technology for people with disabilities has evolved significantly over the last three decades. Now with the emerging depth knowledge in robotics there have been new interventions for people with disabilities to increase access. In order to overcome challenges of persons with disabilities and limited mobility, this project is directed towards utilizing knowledge from assistive technology to make daily life activities easier.


## 1.2 Problem Statement

Eating is a basic activity of daily living, but for individuals with limited upper-limb mobility, it becomes a significant challenge. Conditions such as stroke, cerebral palsy, and spinal cord injuries can make it difficult to pick up food and eat independently. This often leads to stress, social discomfort, and even nutritional risks. Despite advancements in assistive technology, commercially available assistive robotic arms remain expensive and are not widely accessible in our country. This hinder individuals with limited upper-limb mobility from benefiting from such technologies.


## 1.3.1 General Objective 

This project focuses on designing and developing an affordable and efficient assistive robotic arm that supports individuals with limited upper-limb mobility in performing essential daily activities.

## 1.3.2 Specific Objectives

•	Design a functional robotic arm

•	Implement joystick-based manual control

•	Implement voice-command control using Raspberry Pi

•	Demonstrate safe pick-and-place operation


## 1.4	Significance of the Study

This project makes it possible for users such as individuals with disabilities and limited mobility, elderly individuals, and stroke patients to perform daily activities more easily and efficiently. It provides a low-cost solution that enhances independence while reducing stress placed on caregivers, hospitals, and local healthcare and rehabilitation centers.


## 1.5	Scope of Work 

Design, development, and implementation of an affordable assistive robotic arm to aid individuals with limited mobility in performing simple household pick-and-place tasks. The arm is a table-top device controlled through both joystick and voice commands. It features six Degrees of Freedom (6-DOF) to provide sufficient flexibility and functionality and is designed to handle lightweight objects, ensuring reliable and accurate pick-and-place operations.
